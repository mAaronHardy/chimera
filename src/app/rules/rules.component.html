Create a Character

Mobs

Choose a mob (creature) type for your character. The choosen creature determines the starting stats and abilities of your character.

Jobs

Choose a job for your character. The chosen job provides bonus stats, inherent abilities, and unlockable abilities.

While not in combat, characters can freely change between any jobs they have unlocked. Jobs can be unlocked by learning abilities from other jobs, or by purchasing training/license from a trainer.

Stats

Determine stats by adding up mobs stats, job stat bonuses, and level stat bonuses. Strength, Endurance, Dexterity, Agility, Intelligence, Will. At Level 1 add one point to one of your stats.

Combat

Combat consists of rounds. Each round, characters takes a turn to move and perform an action. Rounds are repeated until one team is elminated, or until a unique objective has been completed.

Turn order is determined by which character has the highest agility. Ties are resolved randomly (by coin toss or dice roll).

Movement

Characters can move before and after performing an action, as long as they have the required Mv available and are not otherwise restricted from moving.

Move (Mv)

Characters can move a number of squares equal to their Mv each turn.

If a character still has remaining Mv after moving and performing an action, they can continue to move after their action.

After each move the character determines their facing. Facing can change the accuracy and effect of incoming attacks and techniques.

Jump (Jm)

Moving from one space to another is limited by height.

If the hieght difference of a space is greater than a character's Jump Jm, they cannot move into that space.

If the difference of a lower space is greater than a character's jump, they will recieve damage from moving into that space.
(Hieght Difference x (2 + End))

Swim

When moving from a space with a water height of 2 or greater, a character is considered to be swimming.

Two Mv is required to move to another space while swimming. Characters cannot attack while they are currently swimming.

Techniques such as Swim and Aquaphobia, can affect a characters movement and actions in water.

Actions

Each character can make one action during their turn. Attacks and Techniques can beformed as an action.

Attack

Attacks are actions use to attack a target with an equiped weapon. To attack a character must choose a target that is in range, determined accuracy against the target's defense, then determine damage if the attack hits.

Characters can attack with one weapon they have avaialbe through their Equipement or Techniques.

Characters with multiple weapons available can must choose only one weapon to attack with. A character can only attack with two weapons if they have the Dual Wield technique assigned.

Range

Range is used to determine eligible targets for an attack or technique. The verticle range of all attacks and techniques is equal to their range.

Accuracy

Attack accuracy for weapons is based on Dexterity (Dex).

To determin accuracy, roll a d20 and add the characters Dex to that number.

Accuracy is then compared to the target's Defense, their Block or Dodge (whichever is higher). If the Accuracy is equal to or higher the attack hits.

Block is considered to be 0 when the target is attacked from othe back.

Technique

Their are a wide variety of techniques. Physical techniques work exactly like weapon attacks. While Special techniques are similar, but their success is determined by failure rate instead of accuracy.

Failure Rate

After choosing a target or area for the technique, the attacker then rolls a d20 and adds a number to that based on the attribute associated with the spell. If the roll is equal to or higher than the failure rate plus the target's defense, the attack hits.

Unblockable

Some special techniques cannot be blocked. Block is considered to be 0 when trageting a character with an unblockable attack.

Undodgeable

Some special techniques cannot be dodged. Dodge is considered to be 0 when trageting a character with an undodgeable attack.
